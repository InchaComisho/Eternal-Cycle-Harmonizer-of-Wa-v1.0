## Eternal Cycle — Harmonizer of Wa —  
A Civilization-Tuning Action RPG About Sustainable Worlds

### What Is Eternal Cycle?

**Eternal Cycle** is a game concept that combines:

- Fast-paced 3-character action RPG combat  
- Civilization simulation  
- Time-loop progression  
- Hack-and-slash gameplay  
- Dynamic world evolution  

The player is not a traditional hero.  
You are a **Watcher**, a **Mediator**, and ultimately a **Harmonizer of civilizations**.

The goal is not simply to defeat enemies.  
The true goal is to prevent the world itself from collapsing into the **Void** and to stop the appearance of **The Eternal**.

***

## Core Gameplay

### 3-Character Real-Time Action Combat

Combat is built around a fast and fluid 3-character party system.  
Players control one character at a time while switching between allies during battle.

Core mechanics include:

- Fast enemy clearing  
- BREAK system  
- Perfect dodge  
- QTE support actions  
- Special skills  
- Two-character combo Ultimates  

The game focuses on maintaining combat flow and satisfying action gameplay.

### Enemies Are Designed for Momentum

Regular enemies are **not** intended to feel slow or overly durable.  
The goal is:

- Fast combat flow  
- Large-scale enemy clearing  
- Skill chaining  
- Resource cycling  
- Hack-and-slash satisfaction  

The true difficulty of the game is not surviving combat itself.  
The true difficulty is **maintaining the balance of civilization**.

### Skill System

Each class shares a common combat foundation, but players choose one of four skill directions:

- **Single Target** – High damage against bosses and elite enemies.  
- **Area** – Wide-range attacks focused on crowd clearing.  
- **Drain** – Converts damage into healing and resource circulation.  
- **Reflection** – Automatic defensive and counter-based abilities.  

The selected skill type also changes the behavior of special attacks and Ultimate Skills.

### Two-Character Ultimate System

Ultimate Skills are activated through combinations of **two characters**, instead of requiring all three party members.

This allows:

- Flexible party building  
- More experimentation  
- Easier balancing  
- Distinct visual combinations  
- Faster gameplay flow  

***

## Civilization Tuning System

### The World Is Always Changing

There is no fixed solution or perfect route.  
Every cycle changes:

- Civilization development  
- Resource distribution  
- Diplomacy  
- Ecology  
- Trade  
- Cultural balance  
- Environmental stability  

Players must observe the world and adapt each time.

### Planet-Scale World Map

The world is represented through a planetary civilization map.  
Each region visually changes depending on its condition.

- 🟢 **Green** – Stable and balanced  
- 🟡 **Yellow** – Unstable or under tension  
- 🔴 **Red** – Conflict and collapse in progress  
- ⚫ **Void** – Civilization and natural circulation have stopped  

If left untreated, instability spreads into nearby regions.

### Judgment: Destroy or Forgive

After defeating major enemies or rulers, players must choose:

**Destroy**

- Immediate rewards and powerful materials  

But this may cause:

- Hatred and retaliation  
- Diplomatic collapse  
- Ecological imbalance  
- Expansion of the Void  

**Forgive**

- Long-term stability and coexistence  

But this requires:

- Continuous management  
- Resource investment  
- Ongoing civilization tuning  

The game is not based on simple morality.  
The true danger is **extreme imbalance**.

***

## The Void and The Eternal

### The Void

The Void is not simply darkness or evil.  
It is **civilization-scale circulation failure**.

Examples include:

- Environmental collapse  
- Endless war  
- Isolationism  
- Excessive industrialization  
- Social stagnation  
- Ecological destruction  
- Over-centralized order  

When imbalance reaches critical levels, regions gradually become consumed by the Void.

### The Eternal

The Eternal is not a traditional final boss.  
It is the world's **automatic correction mechanism**—a **planetary immune system**.

When civilization crosses a critical threshold of imbalance, The Eternal appears to forcibly reset the cycle.

The true victory condition is:

> **Preventing the appearance of The Eternal itself.**

***

## Time Loop System

When civilization collapses, the world enters a **time loop**.  
However, the world is never fully reset.

### Before the True Ending

Before achieving the True Ending:

- Equipment resets  
- Civilization rewinds  
- Relationships partially fade  

But **fragments remain**.

Examples include:

- Ancient blueprints  
- Blacksmith knowledge  
- Dreams  
- Déjà vu  
- Ruins from previous timelines  

The world remembers in **echoes**.

### After the True Ending

After reaching the True Ending:

- Equipment is fully retained  
- Levels remain  
- Party members remember past cycles  
- Eternal-themed skins unlock  
- Alternate worldlines become available  

This is where the **true endgame** begins.

### Endgame Structure

- **Continued Civilization Route**  
  Continue developing a stabilized world.

- **Alternate Worldline Route**  
  Enter completely different civilizations and historical outcomes.

Each worldline may contain:

- Different dominant races  
- Different ecological conditions  
- Different ideological imbalances  
- Different forms of collapse  

Enemies scale alongside the player, while loot quality also increases.  
The game fully embraces long-term hack-and-slash progression.

***

## Races

There are no inherently evil races.  
Each race represents a different civilizational philosophy.

- **Humans** – Adaptable and ambitious. Strong in science and industrial growth.  
- **Demons** – Long-lived and powerful. High magical affinity and harmony-oriented societies.  
- **Angels** – Winged beings focused on order and purification.  
- **Elves** – Nature-oriented civilizations emphasizing circulation and coexistence.  
- **Dwarves** – Masters of structure, engineering, and blacksmithing.  
- **Beastfolk** – Mobile and instinctive societies with strong trade cultures.  

***

## The Six Principles

The world is built upon six core principles:

- **Natural Law** – The fundamental structure of existence and causality.  
- **Harmony** – The ability of different beings to coexist.  
- **Circulation** – The continuous flow of life, energy, resources, and civilization.  
- **Structure** – Stable foundations that support society and civilization.  
- **Order** – Systems that prevent collapse and uncontrolled chaos.  
- **Wa** – A higher state of resonance and integrated coexistence beyond simple conflict resolution.  

***

## The Core Theme

Eternal Cycle is not simply about becoming stronger.  
It is about:

- Observing civilization  
- Correcting imbalance  
- Maintaining circulation  
- Preventing collapse  
- Understanding sustainable coexistence  

The player is not a conqueror.  
The player is a **civilization harmonizer**.
